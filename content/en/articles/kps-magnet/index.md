The Karnataka Electricity Consumers Association (KECA) strongly opposes the recent move by Tata Power to seek electricity distribution licenses in multiple circles across Karnataka in 15 Districts. KECA views this as a backdoor entry for full-scale privatization of Electricity, with far-reaching implications for consumers, farmers, and employees in the power sector.

KECA believes that electricity distribution is an essential public service and should primarily function in the larger public interest rather than for commercial profit. Experiences from various parts of the country indicate that privatization in the power distribution sector often leads to increased financial burden on consumers, reduced accountability, and growing uncertainty for employees.

The Association is concerned that private operators will focus mainly on high-revenue urban, industrial, and commercial consumers, leaving state-owned ESCOMs to shoulder the responsibility of serving rural and economically weaker sections. Such a situation could adversely affect the financial sustainability of public utilities and eventually result in higher tariffs and additional charges for ordinary consumers.

KECA demands that the Karnataka Electricity Regulatory Commission (KERC) summarily reject Tata Power's license applications. KECA further appeals to the Government of Karnataka to safeguard the state’s public power infrastructure and clearly affirm its commitment to strengthening the public electricity distribution system.

“Electricity is a basic public necessity and an essential service. It must remain accessible, affordable, and accountable to the people, rather than driven primarily by corporate interests.”

We calls upon consumers, civil society organizations, farmers, and worker unions across Karnataka to come together in protecting the public character of the state’s electricity sector.

Yours sincerely,
 
**V Gnanamurthy**
