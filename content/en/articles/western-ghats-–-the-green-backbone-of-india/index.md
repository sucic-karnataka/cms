The Western Ghats are a magnificent mountain range located along the western edge of the Indian subcontinent, running parallel to the Arabian Sea. Stretching for about 1,600 kilometers, this range passes through several Indian states, including Gujarat, Maharashtra, Goa, Karnataka, Kerala, and Tamil Nadu. [[en.wikipedia.org]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Western_Ghats)

Also known as the “Sahyadri” hills, the Western Ghats form the western boundary of the Deccan Plateau and separate the narrow coastal plains from the interior regions of India. [[britannica.com]](https://www.britannica.com/place/Western-Ghats)

### **A Hub of Biodiversity**

The Western Ghats are recognized as one of the world’s most important biodiversity hotspots. They are home to thousands of plant and animal species, many of which are endemic and found nowhere else on Earth.
Due to their ecological importance, the Western Ghats were designated as a UNESCO World Heritage Site, highlighting their global environmental significance. [[wildlifena...igator.com]](https://wildlifenavigator.com/western-ghats/) [[whc.unesco.org]](https://whc.unesco.org/en/list/1342)

### **Influence on Climate and Rainfall**

This mountain range plays a crucial role in shaping India’s climate, especially during the monsoon season. The Western Ghats act as a barrier that captures moisture-laden winds from the Arabian Sea, resulting in heavy rainfall along the western slopes.
As a result, the coastal regions are lush and green, while the eastern side experiences much less rainfall. [[en.wikipedia.org]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Western_Ghats)

### **Source of Major Rivers**

The Western Ghats serve as the origin of many important rivers in India, including the Godavari, Krishna, and Kaveri. These rivers support millions of people by supplying water for agriculture, drinking, and industry. [[wildlifena...igator.com]](https://wildlifenavigator.com/western-ghats/)

### **Environmental and Social Importance**

The region is not only rich in natural beauty but also plays a vital role in sustaining life. It supports diverse ecosystems, provides medicinal plants, and supplies water resources to millions of people across southern India. [[wwfindia.org]](https://www.wwfindia.org/about_wwf/critical_regions/western_ghats2/about_the_western_ghats/)

### **Need for Conservation**

In recent years, the Western Ghats have been facing threats such as deforestation, urbanization, and climate change. These challenges pose a serious risk to its biodiversity and ecological balance. Therefore, protecting and conserving this natural treasure has become more important than ever. [[wwf.panda.org]](https://wwf.panda.org/discover/knowledge_hub/where_we_work/western_ghats/)




## **Conclusion:**

The Western Ghats are more than just a mountain range—they are a lifeline for India’s environment and people. Preserving this natural heritage is essential for maintaining ecological balance and ensuring a sustainable future.
